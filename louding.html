Synopsis

As a third wife in a small Nigerian village,fourteen year old Adunni is expected to fade into silence. But Aduni will not keep quiet.She's funny and curious, with an infectious joyful spirit.Despite her adversity awaiting her at every turn,she's set on getting an education,no matter the cost.

About Abi Dare

Abimbola "Abi" Daré is a Nigerian author and public speaker who now lives in Essex, England. She was born in Lagos,Nigeria and has a background in law, international project management and creative writing.In 2018, she won the Bath Novel Award, and was a finalist in the Literary Consultancy Pen Factor 2018. Her debut novel The Girl with the Louding Voice was published in 2020.She is the author of many heart winning books like 'The small print', 'You are not going back', 'So I Can Roar'.In 'THE GIRL WITH THE LOUDING VOICE' just like in most of her books explores themes of female empowerment, education and resilince. The character in this book not only wants a loud voice but a louding voice. An ambitious young girl who is not piting herself for her current situation but have dreams and hopes ahead that she is not willing to let go off.Adunni believed that education will give her the voice to navigate the World that tries to silence her.Abi Dare states that "Every story has a start" she has seen this happening and she desired to create a novel that celebrates the power of young women to find their voices and to make a positive impact on the world.

The place of women basically refers to how women are viewed in society when it comes to their role status and position. The book vividly portrays how women are viewed. Faced with challenges, yet unable to voice their concerns due to cultural beliefs that they are nothing compared to men.

Women in this village are being deprived of education, this is because they are viewed as assets who will be married off to men who will provide them with everything they want. We are introduced to a character called Adunni in the book, where she yearns for education but being that she is born a woman, she cannot achieve this because society claims that women are suppose to stay at home and when they reach a certain age they are going to be married off to men who will provide for them. This factor highly affects the girls from her village, where they are forced not to pursue their dreams,but they are encouraged to get married and have children. This factor not only affects Adunni but also some other women in their village. Adunni’s mother was affected by this in that she was forced to get married even before she could complete her education, which led her to get married to a man who made her lack happiness in her life. Another girl who faces this challenge of not being heard is Kiki the daughter to Morufu where she is forced to get married at a younger age having to throw away her dream of being a designer.

In instances where the family is poor, they usually prioritize educating men over women because they believe that a woman would be married to another community, and it would be a poor investment because other people will benefit from their daughter’s education and empowerment. This has affected most communities worldwide where the families experience constraints.

Women in this society are viewed as a source of wealth, which instigates the reason why they are being married off early. In that the parents know that when they are married to rich men, their family will not suffer from financial constraints because they have richin-laws who can fund their lives, and they will be able to get basic supplies. Khadija is one of the culprits who suffered this in that her family married her offto her man because they knew this man would be able to supply them with food and cater for their medical expenses.

The place of women in society

Aduni is also affected when the father used to tell her that if she were married off to a rich man, their family would benefit more because their house rent would be catered for, and they would never lack food. Many families in Africa tend to view women as wealth because when they just hear a family has a daughteror many daughters, they tend to think about how the family will be rich when the dowry is being paid, hence this highly affects the people from this communities hence influencing early marriages in cases where the family wants an easy earned resources.

Women are nothing without men. This is where we are introduced to Florence, yes, she had a husband, but then the husband used to depend on her for everything. She used to help her husband’s family and also give her husband stipends, but this did not make her husband to respect her simply because she is a woman. The husband does not have anything at all because he is being provided with everything by the woman, but then the society fails to acknowledge that because she is a woman, and she relies on her husband for her to be acknowledged in society as an important person.

This is a common scenario in that there is a premonition that women are weak; therefore, they need men in their lives. A common relation point is when we have the election for presidency, if a woman vies and a man also vies, the chances of a woman failing are high because they are viewed to be inferior to men. In as much as there are countries led by women but it really takes a lot to be able to compete with a man in the presidential seat and emerge as winner because the society sees women as somewhat weak and not wise enough to lead a group of men as men tend to think they are highly superior. Even in companies, we tend to find men in the president, or rather the CEO's position, and women the vice president because apparently "women cannot lead men".

Traditional cultural beliefs

"If a woman is born a girl, she is born to suffer"

Cultural beliefs affect people’s way of thinking, and this, in turn, ends up affecting the way people show up in life and the choices they make when they're in difficult situations. They also impact and intersect with spiritual and religious practice. In the book, we see how different people solve their problems using traditional ways, and some of them end up being hurt in the process and lose money too.

For instance, when Khadija feels unwell, she goes to her lover, Bamidele, because she believes that there is a curse upon their family and that bathing in a river seven times with a special soap will make her well, instead of going to the midwife. Bamidele says that every pregnant woman has to take a bath before nine months, or she will die while giving birth. She ends up dying beside the river because “Bamidele did not show up with the soap”.

Tia’s mother-in-law suggests that she should take the bath so that she can have children, and she agrees to this, not because she wants to, but because she wants her to stop pestering her. When she goes to the shrine, she gets beaten, and they end up realizing that the husband is the one who is infertile, and yet the mother-in-law believed that she would get children after visiting the medicine man, and she even paid for it. In the current society in Kenya, especially in the Kisii and Kamba communities, most people believe in curses and traditional medicine. Some people in the community are avoided because they are believed to be witches, and even the people who just moved to that place avoid them without any specific reason as to why they are avoiding them. When a person gets sick, instead of going to the hospital, they go to shrines of certain medicine men,and they believe that it is better than going to the hospital and that they will get better. When a woman has daughters only and wants a son, she goes to the medicine man, and she believes that the next baby she will give birth to will be a boy. It is the same case with barren women; they believe that the medicine man will solve their issue and that they will be able to have children. People also go to the medicine man for general life issues like a lack of money and disputes in the family, because they see this as the best solution to their problems. The medicine men are paid, and the practice is their occupation. They are expensive, and sometimes whatever you went there for doesn’t work, and you end up losing money instead.

Child marriage is a human rights violation taking place on a vast scale in Africa, which disproportionately affects girls. Girls who are married at young age often drop out of school and face physical risks, especially during pregnancy. Due to the social, health, and economic impacts of child marriage, the practice is a major obstacle to sustainable development.

In this book, in the village of Ikati, girls who are not even 15 years old are forced to marry just for the sake of their families to get money for food and rent. The girls have no choice over whom they marry and when to marry and this hinders their educational life because they are sold to men who are older than them for the sake of the survival of their families.

By the story of Adunni, she dropped out of school after the death of her mother and was unable to continue due to the financial issues, and her dad who was a lazy man who didn't want to work, sold her only daughter to Morufu who had three wives, so that he could survive He thought that having a daughter was the only cure to his poverty, and Adunni had no choice but to go and live with a torturous man and other wives.

In Adunni’s village, young girls like her have no other future but to marry. When they turn 14 years old, they are excited to marry because they don’t have any other life apart from being married off to men who are old enough to be their father. This limits these young girls from unlocking their potential and living their lives in general for example in the book there is a girl called khadija who was just married of to a man just because her family wanted a bag of rice and also morufu’s daughter who always dreamed of becoming a designer little did she know that her father saw her as a grown woman and she needed to marry mind you she was only fifteen, a little girl with dreams.

Education

"Education is the key. The key to open the door to all the things I dream."

Education is a central theme of the book, highlighting the struggle of a young Nigerian girl, Adunni, who eagerly wants to gain an education and use her “louding” voice to advocate for herself and others.

The journey started when Aduni’s mother was alive, she taught Adunni that her schooling is her voice. As a result, Adunni spent her life searching for empowerment and self-esteem through education. However, her quest for knowledge was challenged by an abusive man and indentured servitude . Despite the dismal circumstances and the cruel people who belittle her, she refused to stay silent and incurious, instead she was finding ways to educate herself through small unintentional opportunities and passing the power of voice along to others. Throughout her journey the novel suggested that education uniquely empowers one’s self worth, self awareness, confidence, and independence.

Adunni’s mother influenced Adunni to acquire much knowledge which allowed her to be active and purposeful to her surroundings. Her ability to use unintentional opportunities helped her to read two books ( The Collins English Dictionary and The Book of Nigerian Facts) when she was working as a housemaid for big madam. Adunni consults these books frequently, using the dictionary to learn new words and The Book of Nigerian Facts to learn more about Nigerian culture and society. Those whole experiences imbued Adduni with the strength and self-assurance she needs to go forward to live with her dream-being a teacher.
