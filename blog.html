Family Love

The family love is what held Patricia’s family together because when Patricia was certain that she wanted a baby, the main reason was that she wanted someone who would love her unconditionally. The upbringing of Trevor was as a form of love because his mother used to support him, teaching him the skills required for him to have a good life, and he used to discipline him so that he would have good manners. Patricia was very keen when it came to her son because he was dedicated to giving him the best of the best. Patricia did not want her son to miss anything this is due to the reason because he used to try and take him to the amusement park so that he would not miss the joy of childhood. Family love enabled Trevor to be able to talk to his dad even after not being part of her life for such a long time. They were able to rekindle their relationship. When Trevor’s mother was sick, Trevor was not at their home. When he was called, he was able to go and pay his mom's medical fees. What led to her mom being hospitalized? She was protecting her son from being shot by the “husband”. This is a true image of love for family.

Explaining the quote Abel wanted a traditional marriage with a traditional wife...but he never fell in love with subservient women...'He only wants a woman who is free because his dream is to put her in a cage.'" The kind of wife that Abel a subservient wanted is nearly extinct because nowadays the women who are present are trying to stand up for themselves and make their own decisions. They do not need men who are going to monitor and command them around. The days are long gone when women would be left in the house to tend to house chores, forsaking their careers for family’s sake, allowing the husband to continue with their careers. Nowadays, women are empowered and they know what they want and can stand in a position to challenge men. Sometimes back, there were roles delegated to men and some to women. For instance, technical jobs were mostly left for men, but nowadays we are seeing this tradition being abandoned because women are in a position to challenge men, leading to fair competition in the industry. Careers like engineering were left for men, but nowadays every field of engineering has a representation of a woman. As they are not proportional, but there is some effort made, and soon the ratio will be proportional. When it comes to role swapping, nowadays we can easily find men who can do house chores efficiently even when the wife is present. This is something that was not there, but now it is embraced as women can stand for themselves. This is termed as empowerment and having a being recognized in the future we will have. Apart from that, women need to have their own money and be independent; henc,e the chances of such women being available are very low because the women are trying to empower each other.

Written by Gladwine

A mother's Love

As much as the book revolves around Trevor and his experience as a mixed person living in apartheid, it is clear as day how far this mother was ready to go to give him a life she never had, to open his mind up to greater things and opportunities. She opened a new world of possibilities by giving Trevor a different perspective on life. Patricia worked numerous jobs to take him to school and secure his future. She bought him books to read and improve his English, taught him how to treat women with respect, and taught him how to have a relationship with God. Patricia disciplined Trevor from a place of love to ensure her child doesn't get disciplined by the world for the same mistakes. Patricia never let them sleep hungry, even when she was short on money, she found a way to put something on the table. She was selfless and ready to do anything for her children. Patricia let Trevor walk away from her when her relationship with Abel started becoming toxic for him to stay. She put him ahead of herself even when she lay in a hospital bed with a gunshot wound to the head, yet she would ask him not to worry about her. She was willing to request a coloured woman to walk next to her son as she walked in the back like a maid, just to give Trevor a chance to play and see the outside. She displays the purest form of love that can only be found in a mother.

Reflective blog

A submissive woman in this day and age has become a thing of the past. Women don't bow down to greet their husbands when they come home; they go to offices and work just as hard as men do, and even outshine them. Women have broken barriers that existed in the past, they are leaders, Engineers, Doctors, and Lawyers. Women have fought for the independence they have gained over time it is no longer dominated by men. But, society has placed women in predefined boxes; if these expectations go unchecked, a woman has officially failed in life. This biased way of living is backward and has been passed by time. While women no longer worship their husbands or submit to their every wish, the modern way of thinking is just as degrading as it was in the past, only in a different time and way. Even though women have made milestones in society, they still lose to men, it is never a race of equals, one always has an upper hand. We see these instances in different things that are not spoken about, like when a woman is not married or having a child at a certain age, society silently judges them, declaring them as failures, regardless of their successes or accomplishments. This would not be the case if the roles were to be reversed and a man were the one to do the same. As much as things have changed and women have more going on for them, it is fair to say men still have the upper hand, even in workplaces, for instance where women find they have to work twice as hard as men only to achieve less pay compared to men. Women have proven to be more competent than men, yet the odds are still stacked against them.

The Cage: Expectations, Freedom, and Control Relationships

Abel wanted a traditional marriage with a traditional wife...but he never fell in love with subservient women...'He only wants a woman who is free because his dream is to put her in a cage.'" The quote deeply describes the exposing paradox of relationships today. It clearly shows a desire for control masked in the name of love. I can briefly explain this in the context of my upbringing as a village girl from the northern part of Ethiopia. Growing up in my village, community expectations of women were for them to perform household chores. Marriages were usually arranged without the consent of the woman and her value was judged by her ability to bear children and by the way she cared for her family especially for her husband. The words "Freedom and Power" were far from existence in the heads of the women in our village. Despite thoseconstraints, some women were defending their individuality, intelligence, and desires. They figured out ways to satisfy their desires and wants.

Abel's desire clearly shows the disturbing truth, that the desire for control and power can disguise itself as love and as longing for one's partner. Trying to break women's spirit, and diminishing them in a cage where men dominated has been a reality for years. When we think of today's world, this phenomenon is happening in relationship dynamics, in the workplace, and in different societal participation, though it has decreased to some extent. The cage is not only a physical space but it also includes the emotional, psychological, and financial situations. As for me, the message I got from this quote is that the struggle for equality and true partnership requires courage to break the shadows of the past and to stand strong in hardships.

From the Black, White and Beyond

If I could give “Born a Crime” a new title, it would be “ From the Black, White and Beyond: Navigating a divided world.” While the existing title is undeniably impactful in drawing attention to the illegality of his birth, “From the Black, White and Beyond: navigating a divided world” better describes the reality of race in shaping Trevor’s early life. The phrases “black” and “white” literally evoke the racial classifications of apartheid. Metaphorically, “black and white” refers to the good and the bad, representing Trevor’s experience of both sides of the world. However “and beyond” is crucial because it speaks to his mixed race identity.

A personal blog on reflecting to a quote from “Born a Crime”

"Abel wanted a traditional marriage with a traditional wife but he never fell in love with a subservient woman, He only wants a woman who is free because his dream is to put her in a cage" The quote is a strange and a very sad thought about how some people perceive most relationships. It shows how they want to control someone, in the name of love. This quote highlights how easily people fall into social aspects that value a woman that is “free and independent” until she has a husband. Then she has to be fully controlled. We see this happening today in many ways. Think about a woman who has a good job, but everyone expects her to prioritize her husband's career over her own. Or a mom who stays home with her kids, but people judge her for not having her own career. It's the idea that a woman should be primarily defined by her relationship and her home life, not for who she is as a person. From my own experience, I have seen this play out with female family members who were initially praised for being ground breakers in their careers, only to face pressure to conform to traditional roles once married. Their individuality became a source of tension and conflict. Abel’s desire for traditional marriage is not inherently wrong, the contradiction lies in wanting a free-spirited partner only to then suppress that spirit. This quote serves as a reminder of the importance of recognizing and challenging these paradoxical expectations that limit both men and women, and ultimately poison relationships.

written by Tirsit

I would name the book ” Finding Yourself”.

From the book we see the journey of Trevor Noah as he tries to fit in and wondering why he is different until he found out who he wanted to be. At one time, he even asks his mother why they never went to Germany because other white kids had told him that they went to Germany because of the apartheid. He thought that if he had gone to Germany, he would fit in without the struggle he was going through in their country. In school he was confused as to where he should go during break because students were classified according to colour and he did not fit to either of the extremes. He was too light for the blacks and he was too black for the whites. In the process he learned to use language as he had observed his mother do. He found out that language more than skin can be used to define who you are. He used to speak the Xhosa, English,Zulu and Afrikaans. He used to talk to the students in their languages and as a result students started interacting with him and this made him less of an outsider. Through his interaction with the black students he realized that he wanted to be black as compared to being mixed or white. Even when the school insisted that he should stay with the white people he fought to stay with the black students. He accepted the fact that the only way he could interact with the females was through being funny and he was comfortable with this. With time he gained confidence after his acne wore off.

Relationship expectations and gender roles

The quote resonates with relationship expectations in today’s society because some men choose to marry women who are independent and free spirited just to make them submissive and exercise their power over them. The sole reason they go after such women is because they want to make them change to what the society expects and not because they love them. This is what attracts them to a woman and this is also the reason why they cannot go for the already submissive women; they want to change the women to be submissive to them and break their beliefs so that they can conform to societal expectations. At first the men will praise the independence, ambition and intelligence of the women and once they are together, they would start talking against the same things they were praising. They would start verbal abuse as a joke which would sometimes develop into physical abuse. They would even go ahead and tell them to stop working so that they can take care of the kids at home and they expect the woman to obey without any question. This brings in the gender roles associated with women. The women according to the society are supposed to be submissive and obedient to the husband. They are also expected to stay at home with the kids, do all the cleaning and the laundry while the man is at work. The men on the other hand are believed to be the head of the family and so they expect whatever they say to be followed with no questions even when it is not favouring the wife. He does not take into account what the wife wants. At some instances the woman becomes so dependent on the man that she cannot leave him and ends conforming to the society.
